# Chapter 4: Complex Numbers & Quadratic Equations - Important Questions

## 1 Mark Questions
1. **Question:** Express $i^9 + i^{19}$ in the form $a + ib$.
   **Answer:** $0 + i(0) = 0$ (Since $i^9 = i$ and $i^{19} = -i$)
2. **Question:** Find the multiplicative inverse of $2 - 3i$.
   **Answer:** $\frac{2}{13} + i\frac{3}{13}$

## 2 Mark Questions
1. **Question:** Solve the quadratic equation $x^2 + 3x + 9 = 0$.
   **Answer:** $x = \frac{-3 \pm 3\sqrt{3}i}{2}$
2. **Question:** Express in polar form: $1 + i\sqrt{3}$.
   **Answer:** $2(\cos\frac{\pi}{3} + i\sin\frac{\pi}{3})$

## 3 Mark Questions
1. **Question:** Convert $\frac{-16}{1 + i\sqrt{3}}$ into polar form.
   **Answer:** $8(\cos\frac{2\pi}{3} + i\sin\frac{2\pi}{3})$
2. **Question:** Solve $x^2 - (3\sqrt{2} - 2i)x - 6\sqrt{2}i = 0$.
   **Answer:** $x = 3\sqrt{2}$ or $x = -2i$

## 5 Mark Questions
1. **Question:** If $(x + iy)^3 = u + iv$, then show that $\frac{u}{x} + \frac{v}{y} = 4(x^2 - y^2)$.
   **Answer:** Expand $(x+iy)^3$, equate real/imaginary parts to $u$ and $v$, substitute and simplify.
